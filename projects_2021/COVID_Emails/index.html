How Yale talked about COVID

The words admin and HOCs used to talk to students about the pandemic

Analysis by Leon Lufkin

As the semester comes to a close, the pandemic seems to be nearing its end too. Vaccines are going into people's arms and a return to normal life is on the horizon.

Here, we take a look at how Yale's heads of college (HOCs) and adminstrators talked to students about this defining moment in history. We gathered all the emails they've sent since the start of the pandemic to analyze the words they used.

Words on the left were used more frequently by HOCs, and those on the right were used more by administrators.

Hover over a word to get started!

About this project

This is a project of the Yale Daily News Data Desk, one of the newest desks that aims to bring data-driven journalism to the News.

The News gathered 48 emails sent by administrators and HOCs from each of the 14 colleges since the start of the pandemic. These emails were analyzed in R to identify common words and compare their usage. Email Leon Lufkin if you think we missed any!

You can find our scripts, code, and cleaned dataset on GitHub.
